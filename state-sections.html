History

Since the 1930's, South Dakota was widely known for its population decline due to the Dust Bowl and a combination of economic and climatic conditions. However, economic stability returned with the U.S. entry into World War II and the implementation of the Pick Sloan Plan in 1944, which resulted in the construction of dams providing flood control, hydroelectricity, and recreational opportunities.

Now, South Dakota has transitioned from an agriculture-dominated state to a more diversified economy, with significant growth in the tourism and financial service industries. Nevertheless, South Dakota often ranks highly for its quality of life, being named the happiest, healthiest state in the United States.
